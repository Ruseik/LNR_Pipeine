The final analytical workflow compiled all validated outputs into publication-ready tables and figures after completion of the earlier preprocessing and modeling steps. The study cohort was restricted to node-positive colorectal cancer patients with valid lymph node and survival data, and the reporting set included overall survival (OS) and disease-specific survival (DSS) events. A CONSORT-style patient flow diagram was generated to document exclusions and derive the final analytic cohort. Survival analyses were performed using Kaplan–Meier estimation with log-rank testing for unadjusted comparisons, followed by multivariable Cox proportional hazards regression models to estimate adjusted hazard ratios (HRs) with 95% confidence intervals (CIs). Model discrimination was compared using the concordance statistic (C-statistic) with 95% CIs across sequential modeling layers, including unadjusted, TLE-adjusted, and fully adjusted models. Reclassification between conventional nodal stage and lymph node ratio (LNR) categories was summarized using cross-tabulation to evaluate discordant risk classification. Composite survival figures were prepared by plotting Kaplan–Meier curves for OS stratified by N-stage and LNR tier. In addition, subgroup and sensitivity analyses were conducted to assess robustness across clinically relevant strata, and cancer-specific survival was evaluated using competing-risks methodology to account for non-cancer deaths. All outputs were formatted into manuscript-ready tables and figures for publication.
