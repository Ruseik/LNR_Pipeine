Performance of the multivariable survival models was evaluated using a prespecified validation framework. Discrimination was quantified with Uno’s C-statistic, calculated from the linear predictor with inverse probability of censoring weighting, and model C versus model D was compared using the absolute difference in C-statistics (ΔC) with bootstrap bias-corrected and accelerated (BCa) 95% confidence intervals obtained from resampling. Clinical utility was assessed by decision-curve analysis (DCA) at 5-year overall survival, using predicted 5-year event risks derived from the Cox proportional hazards models. Model calibration at 5 years was examined with bootstrap-based calibration plots generated from the model calibration procedure, and internal validity was assessed with bootstrap optimism-corrected discrimination from bootstrap validation. Overall model fit was compared using the Akaike information criterion (AIC) and Bayesian information criterion (BIC). In exploratory supplementary analyses only, net reclassification improvement (NRI) and integrated discrimination improvement (IDI) were calculated to describe incremental reclassification performance. Where permitted by the analytic tier, restricted cubic splines were used for nonlinear specification of continuous predictors within the Cox models.
