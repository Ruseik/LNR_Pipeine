The analytic cohort included 62 patients, of whom 34 experienced an overall survival event. Cross-tabulation of pathological N stage by LNR tier showed that among 41 patients with N1 disease, 7 (17.1%) were in the low LNR tier, 27 (65.9%) were in the intermediate tier, and 7 (17.1%) were in the high tier. Among 21 patients with N2 disease, none were in the low tier, 2 (9.5%) were in the intermediate tier, and 19 (90.5%) were in the high tier. Overall, 26 patients (41.9%) were classified as concordant between N stage and LNR risk grouping, whereas 36 patients (58.1%) were reclassified as discordant.

Survival analysis by N stage × LNR cell demonstrated marked heterogeneity across groups. Within the N1 subgroup, the intermediate LNR tier contained the largest number of patients (n = 27) and had 13 deaths, with a median survival of 948 days. The N1 low-LNR and high-LNR cells each included 7 patients; median survival was 1131 days in the low-LNR cell and 2290 days in the high-LNR cell. In the N2 subgroup, the high-LNR cell included 19 patients and 13 deaths, with a median survival of 558 days, while the N2 intermediate-LNR cell contained 2 patients and 2 deaths, with a median survival of 793 days.

Restricted mean survival time (RMST) at 5 years also varied across reclassification cells. RMST was 4.08 years for N1/high LNR, 3.35 years for N1/low LNR, 3.18 years for N1/intermediate LNR, 2.48 years for N2/high LNR, and 2.17 years for N2/intermediate LNR. The small number of patients in the N2 intermediate-LNR cell should be considered when interpreting these estimates.

Taken together, these findings indicate substantial discordance between anatomic nodal stage and LNR-based risk classification, with more than half of patients falling into reclassified groups and clear survival separation across the combined N stage × LNR categories.
