Pre-specified subgroup and sensitivity analyses were performed to evaluate the robustness and clinical heterogeneity of the prognostic effects of lymph node ratio (LNR) and nodal stage on overall survival (OS). Subgroup analyses were conducted according to lymph node retrieval adequacy (TLE <12 vs ≥12), tumor T stage (T3/T4 vs T1/T2), and nodal stage (N1 vs N2). Within each subgroup, univariable Cox proportional hazards regression models were fitted for nodal stage and LNR, and model discrimination was quantified using the concordance statistic (C-index/Harrell’s concordance). Kaplan–Meier survival curves were generated for LNR categories within relevant subgroups, and between-group differences were assessed using the log-rank test. A formal effect-modification analysis was planned by fitting an interaction model including LNR and binary TLE adequacy (LNR × TLE adequacy) in the Cox framework when the event count permitted. Sensitivity analyses included a 6-month landmark analysis, in which patients with survival time ≤6 months were excluded and follow-up time was recalculated from the landmark to reduce potential influence of early postoperative mortality; Cox models and Kaplan–Meier analyses were then repeated in the landmark cohort. Additional sensitivity analyses replaced continuous or spline-modeled TLE with a binary TLE variable in multivariable Cox models, and alternative LNR categorizations were tested using cutpoints of 0.05/0.40 and a binary threshold of 0.20. For each LNR specification, survival distributions were compared using Kaplan–Meier analysis and the log-rank test, and predictive performance was compared across categorical and continuous LNR forms using the concordance statistic.
