Reclassification analysis was performed by cross-tabulating pathological N stage against lymph node ratio (LNR) tier to quantify the distribution of patients across risk categories, with results reported as counts, row percentages, and column percentages. Patients were then classified into concordant and discordant reclassification groups based on the expected risk relationship between N stage and LNR: for N1 disease, low and intermediate LNR were considered concordant and high LNR was considered upward reclassification, whereas for N2 disease, high LNR was considered concordant and low or intermediate LNR was considered downward reclassification. The proportion of concordant versus reclassified patients was calculated for the full analytic cohort. Overall survival was subsequently evaluated within each N stage × LNR cell using Kaplan–Meier survival analysis, and survival curves were compared using the log-rank test; cells with very small sample size or no events were excluded from curve estimation. To provide a complementary measure of survival experience, restricted mean survival time (RMST) was estimated for each cell up to a prespecified truncation time, defined as the minimum of the protocol-specified RMST horizon and the maximum observed follow-up. Finally, a heatmap was generated to display the frequency of patients in each N stage × LNR tier cell together with the corresponding overall mortality rate, providing a visual summary of the reclassification pattern and associated survival burden.
