The final analytic cohort comprised 62 node-positive colorectal cancer patients after exclusion of node-negative cases, with no exclusions for missing lymph node or survival data. The cohort included 34 deaths from any cause (54.8%) and 24 cancer-specific deaths (38.7%), and the median follow-up time estimated by the reverse Kaplan–Meier method was 2731 days (7.5 years). The proxy measure for cancer-specific survival data completeness was 70.6%, indicating that disease-specific survival interpretation may be limited by incomplete cause-of-death attribution.

Baseline characteristics were summarized overall and stratified by lymph node ratio (LNR) tier and N stage using standardized mean differences rather than hypothesis testing. The mean age was 60.26 years (SD 12.37), 69.4% of patients were female, 67.7% had T3 disease, 88.7% had low-grade tumours, and 46.8% had lymphovascular invasion. The median total lymph nodes examined (TLE) was 15.0 (IQR 10.5–21.75), the median number of positive nodes was 2.0 (IQR 1.0–4.0), and the median follow-up time in the descriptive table was 1058 days (IQR 336.25–2514.75).

TLE in the node-positive cohort had a mean of 17.0 nodes (SD 9.8), a median of 15.0 nodes (IQR 10.5–21.75), and a range of 1–50 nodes. Using the AJCC minimum threshold, 16 of 62 patients (25.8%; 95% CI 16.6%–37.9%) had inadequate harvests defined as fewer than 12 nodes examined, while 8 of 62 patients (12.9%; 95% CI 6.7%–23.4%) had fewer than 8 nodes examined under the more stringent threshold.

The median LNR was 0.1333 (IQR 0.0748–0.4621), with a mean of 0.3125 (SD 0.3324) and a range of 0.02–1.00. According to the Rosenberg classification, 7 patients (11.3%) were in the low-LNR group (<0.05), 29 (46.8%) were in the intermediate group (0.05–0.20), and 26 (41.9%) were in the high-LNR group (>0.20). Across LNR tiers, TLE decreased and LNR increased substantially in the high-LNR group, which also contained a greater proportion of patients with inadequate nodal harvest.

When stratified by N stage, 41 patients were classified as N1 and 21 as N2. Patients with N2 disease had a higher median number of positive nodes [6.0 (IQR 4.0–9.0) vs 1.0 (IQR 1.0–2.0)] and a higher median LNR [0.50 (IQR 0.33–0.93) vs 0.10 (IQR 0.06–0.13)] than those with N1 disease. In the cross-tabulation of N stage and LNR tier, all 7 patients in the low-LNR category were N1, whereas 19 of 21 N2 patients (90.5%) were in the high-LNR category, indicating strong concordance between higher nodal burden and higher LNR classification.
