Patients were first subjected to data cleaning and cohort selection before analysis. Records with empty identifiers, duplicated patient IDs, implausible lymph node counts (positive lymph nodes greater than total examined lymph nodes), or missing survival information were excluded. Missingness was assessed for all key analytical variables using a variable-level missing data summary, and complete-case analysis was used as the primary approach; however, multiple imputation by chained equations (MICE) was prespecified for downstream analyses if any variable exceeded the missingness threshold. The analytic cohort was then restricted to node-positive colorectal cancer patients using the prespecified filter strategy, and patients without a calculable total lymph node estimate were excluded from lymph node ratio (LNR) models. LNR was recalculated from raw counts as the proportion of positive lymph nodes to total lymph nodes and then categorized using the Rosenberg LNR cut-points into predefined tiers, with additional sensitivity categorizations using alternative binary and three-level thresholds. Tumor stage was grouped as T1/T2, T3, and T4, N stage was coded as N1 versus N2, lymph node yield was classified as adequate or inadequate using the conventional ≥12-node threshold and a stricter ≥8-node sensitivity threshold, and grade, lymphovascular invasion, and sex were recoded as categorical variables. Survival time was retained in days and additionally converted to months and years for reporting. Finally, event counts for overall survival and disease-specific survival were used for analytic tiering to guide downstream multivariable modeling complexity and ensure appropriate model specification.
