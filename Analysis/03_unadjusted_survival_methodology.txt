Overall survival (OS) and disease-specific survival (DSS) were analyzed using time-to-event methods. Kaplan–Meier survival curves were constructed for OS according to N stage and lymph node ratio (LNR) tier, with between-group comparisons performed using the log-rank test; for LNR tier, pairwise log-rank comparisons were additionally conducted with Bonferroni correction for multiple testing. Survival probabilities were summarized at 1-, 3-, and 5-year landmark time points with 95% confidence intervals. Restricted mean survival time (RMST) was estimated up to 5 years, using the lesser of the prespecified truncation time and the maximum observed follow-up, and was calculated separately within each N-stage and LNR-tier subgroup. To assess the functional form of LNR as a continuous exposure, martingale residuals from a null Cox proportional hazards model were plotted against LNR proportion and smoothed using LOESS to evaluate linearity versus threshold or plateau behavior. Univariate Cox proportional hazards regression models were then fitted for N stage, continuous LNR, categorical LNR tier, and other clinicopathologic covariates, reporting hazard ratios with 95% confidence intervals. Where permitted by the analytic tier, LNR was additionally modeled using restricted cubic splines (RCS) with three knots, and non-linearity was assessed using the model’s analysis of variance. DSS was analyzed analogously with Kaplan–Meier methods when the number of DSS events was sufficient. All analyses were performed in R.
