Baseline and outcome characteristics were summarized descriptively in the final node-positive colorectal cancer cohort. Continuous variables were presented as mean (standard deviation) or median (interquartile range) as appropriate, and categorical variables as counts and percentages. Baseline characteristics were tabulated using the CreateTableOne framework, with comparisons stratified by lymph node ratio (LNR) tier and N stage; standardized mean differences (SMDs) were reported to assess balance, and hypothesis testing/p-values were intentionally suppressed. Total lymph nodes examined (TLE) and LNR were characterized using distribution summaries, histograms, and boxplots, with TLE adequacy evaluated against prespecified thresholds and corresponding proportions estimated with Wilson score 95% confidence intervals. The distribution of LNR was additionally categorized using Rosenberg-tier cut points. Event outcomes for overall survival and disease-specific survival were summarized as event counts and proportions, and median follow-up was estimated using the reverse Kaplan–Meier method. A cross-tabulation of N stage by LNR tier was also produced to describe the relationship between conventional nodal staging and LNR-based classification.
